Media effects

by Eric A. Meyer
If you've ever taken a Web design and tried to print it out, you've probably been disappointed. Between browser limitations and the problems inherent in trying to print a complicated design, the paper version never quite looks as good as we'd like. Some sites even produce separate versions of articles, linking to them with a "printer-friendly" claim. Now CSS allows you to restyle one document for different media, freeing you from the need to recreate a page just for printing purposes. Eric A. Meyer, author of Cascading Style Sheets: The Definitive Guide, shows you how.
